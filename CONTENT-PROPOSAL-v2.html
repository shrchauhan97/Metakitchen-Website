3. How we test (/science)

[Route stays /science. Page title becomes "How we test" — sounds like fifty engineers documenting their work, not a marketer's "Science page."]

Hero

Tag: How we test

Headline: One loaf.

Sub: The Daily White was sent to FARE Labs in Gurugram. The standard predictive glycemic index protocol. The number came back at 38.

What the lab does

A predictive Glycemic Index test runs the food through a controlled laboratory model of digestion and measures how fast it releases glucose. The result is converted into a GI value via the Goñi et al. regression — a peer-reviewed methodology used by the FAO and food research bodies worldwide.

Foods below GI 55 are "low." Between 55 and 70 is "medium." Above 70 is "high." White bread sits around 70. Watermelon is 76. An apple is 36. Lentils are around 30.

The Daily White came back at 38.

[NOTE: this is the "honest about edges" pass. "Predictive" is named once. The methodology is named (Goñi et al.) without overclaiming a clinical trial. The number stands.]

The curve (keep)

What this means

  • Lower peak. Blood sugar rises less steeply after the Daily White than after regular bread.
  • Steadier morning. Less of the crash that follows a high-GI breakfast.
  • For people managing blood sugar: a better staple. For diabetics on medication, your physician's guidance is what matters — bread is bread, not a treatment.

Questions we get

Is this medical bread? No. It is bread that has been lab-tested for its glycemic index. Useful for blood-sugar-aware eating; not a treatment.

Is "low GI" the same as "low carb"? No. The Daily White is a normal-carb bread whose carbs release slowly. It is not keto. It is not low-carb.

Will the GI change batch to batch? The recipe and process are fixed. We re-test after any material recipe change, and on every new SKU before it ships.

What about the other loaves? Chia Ciabatta, sourdough variants, jalapeño cheese — and what follows bread — all go through the same protocol before they sit on a shelf.

Why FARE Labs? They are an ISO-accredited Indian food testing lab that runs GI protocols against international standards. Local data, international rigor.

How she works (3 cards — real scope, not the 1% scope)

  • A conversation, not a chatbot.
    She does not behave like a search box. She listens, asks, reasons. She watches the way trust builds between her and the person she is talking to and adjusts her pace. The line from "tell me about your morning" to "should I try a low-GI bread" gets walked over weeks, not seconds.
  • The way India actually speaks.
    Hindi, English, the mix in between. She follows the language you used last, not the one you picked in a dropdown. Voice, text, whichever phone you have. The mirroring is the magic — not the default.
  • She remembers you. Specifically.
    Every conversation adds context. Week three knows what week one did not. She picks up not just on what you ate, but on who shops in your house, who decides, what your mother's blood sugar has been doing, why the loaf might end up in the lunchbox instead of on the table. The household is the user, not just the individual.
