Or change the name.

If "MetaKitchen" is up for debate — here are six naming directions that lean harder into the science/techie energy you mentioned. Each is gut-checked for domain availability, pronunciation, and ownership of the space.

Direction A · Drop "Kitchen"

Meta.

/'me·tə/ · 4 letters

Lean into the techie short form. Bread is the first product, not the brand. Brand becomes a platform name — works as MetaKitchen grows into other staples.

memorablescalable.com taken (Facebook)
Direction B · Compound

Metagrain

/'me·tə·greɪn/ · 9 letters

Locks the category (grain/staples) into the name. Reads as biotech-adjacent without being clinical. Sounds like a research lab + a bakery had a child.

category-definingbrandable
Direction C · Sanskrit science

Anna.

/'ə·nə/ · 4 letters

Sanskrit "अन्न" — food, grain, sustenance. Used in "Anna" of "Annapurna" (goddess of food). One word, deeply Indian, instantly understood, ships globally.

indic rootshortpoeticname conflicts
Direction D · Lab + craft

Khameer

/khə·'meer/ · 7 letters

Hindi/Urdu for "yeast" — the alive ingredient that makes bread bread. Sounds ancient, reads modern. Memorable consonants. No competitor owns it in the wellness staple space.

indic rootcategory fitownable
Direction E · Math + body

Index.

/'in·deks/ · 5 letters

From "Glycemic Index" — the metric you're solving. Doubles as the "index" of a magazine (editorial fit). Implies science without saying "science."

conceptualeditorialgeneric
Direction F · Keep MetaKitchen

MetaKitchen

/'me·tə·'ki·tʃən/ · 11 letters

Don't change the name. Already has signal across decks, domain (.io), entity registration. The redesign carries the meta-shift — the name doesn't have to.
